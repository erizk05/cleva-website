Infrastructure & Maintenance FAQ

Addressing core commercial questions about zero-emission integration, depot charging scaling, security certifications, and high-uptime maintenance schedules.

Our fleets natively support both high-availability Level 2 (AC) overnight chargers and heavy DC Fast Charging (CCS Type 1). For larger depot deployments, our dedicated grid engineering team coordinates directly with local power utilities. We provide pre-packaged modular charging hardware, specify substation requirements, and deploy smart telematics pricing algorithms to schedule charging periods during low-demand off-peak hours, saving thousands in operational utility surge rates.

By removing internal combustion engine elements, CLEVA commercial platforms have roughly 65% fewer moving wear components. Scheduled preventative maintenance is optimized every 20,000 miles, focusing largely on suspension linkages, HVAC coolant loops, cabin air filters, and tire rotation. All diagnostics can be retrieved remotely via our fleet manager API or locally through standard high-voltage service lanes. Local technicians can be certified through our 3-day on-site training course, or you can utilize our 24/7 mobile response crew.

Every commercial chassis we engineer includes a comprehensive 8-Year / 200,000-Mile high-voltage traction battery warranty, guaranteeing at least 80% State of Health (SoH) throughout that period. Through our active battery management system, we monitor pack telemetry in real-time to balance cells and prevent early thermal degradation. When batteries eventually drop below 80% capacity, we coordinate a closed-loop recycling program, repurposing retired battery modules for static grid warehouse energy storage (BESS).

Extreme winter profiles can impact electric driving range as chemical reactions slow down. However, CLEVA integrates an efficient high-performance heat pump and standard thermal pre-conditioning software in all battery modules. By using the telematics dashboard to pre-heat the passenger compartment and battery pack while still connected to depot charging stations, actual driving range is kept within 85-90% of nominal figures even in freezing climates.
